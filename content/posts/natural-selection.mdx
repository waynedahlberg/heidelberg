<p className="flush drop-cap">How will the struggle for existence act in regard to variation? Can the principle of selection, which we have seen is so potent in the hands of man, apply in nature? I think we shall see that it can act most effectually.<Sidenote>The first edition of 1,250 copies sold out on publication day, 24 November 1859. Darwin had compressed twenty years of notebooks into what he called “one long argument.”</Sidenote> Let it be borne in mind in what an endless number of strange peculiarities our domestic productions, and, in a lesser degree, those under nature, vary; and how strong the hereditary tendency is.</p>

Let it be borne in mind how infinitely complex and close-fitting are the mutual relations of all organic beings to each other and to their physical conditions of life.
